# 2011 Rugby World Cup

**Category**: Society & Economics
**Key figures**: New Zealand captain Richie McCaw, coach Graham Henry, fly-half Stephen Donald, prop Tony Woodcock; France captain Thierry Dusautoir

## Summary

The 2011 Rugby World Cup was the seventh edition of rugby union's premier international tournament, hosted by New Zealand from September 9 to October 23, 2011. Twenty national teams competed across the country, culminating in a final at Eden Park in Auckland in which the host nation, the All Blacks, defeated France 8–7 to claim the Webb Ellis Cup.

The victory ended a 24-year wait for New Zealand, which had won the inaugural World Cup in 1987 — also at Eden Park, also against France — but had failed to lift the trophy in the six tournaments since, despite a long record as the sport's dominant side. For the host country, where rugby is a central element of national identity, the win was a major cultural moment.

## The Tournament

Twenty teams contested the tournament, divided into four pools of five before a knockout stage. New Zealand entered as favorites and went through the pool phase unbeaten, defeating France along the way; France, by contrast, lost two pool matches, including that defeat to the hosts, yet recovered to reach the final through the knockout rounds. The tournament was staged against the backdrop of New Zealand's recovery from the February 2011 Christchurch earthquake, which forced matches scheduled for that city to be relocated.

## The Final

The final, played on October 23, 2011, was a tense, low-scoring contest and finished 8–7 — the lowest aggregate score in any Rugby World Cup final. Each side scored a single try: prop Tony Woodcock crossed for New Zealand, while France's captain Thierry Dusautoir scored theirs, with François Trinh-Duc adding the conversion. The decisive points came from the boot of Stephen Donald, New Zealand's fourth-choice fly-half, who had been called into the squad late in the tournament amid a spate of injuries and kicked the second-half penalty that proved the winning margin. France pressed hard in the closing stages but could not find the score to overturn the one-point deficit.

## Significance

New Zealand's win, under captain Richie McCaw and coach Graham Henry, released decades of national anxiety over the All Blacks' repeated failures to convert their status as the world's top-ranked team into World Cup titles. Dusautoir, despite finishing on the losing side, was named the tournament's player of the year for a commanding performance in the final. The event set attendance and broadcast records for a Rugby World Cup held in New Zealand and reaffirmed the sport's standing in the Southern Hemisphere. It stands among 2011's defining sporting occasions, alongside India's home victory at that year's Cricket World Cup.
